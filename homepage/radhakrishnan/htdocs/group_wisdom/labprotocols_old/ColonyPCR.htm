Colony PCR

 

1)           Pick healthy looking individual colonies from the agar plate with the inoculating loop, and transfer the bacteria into a 1.5 mL tube containing 50 mLof water.

2)           Mix gently (vortex slowly or use a pipet tip)

3)           Spot 1 mL of each onto a fresh LB plate (containing the appropriate antibiotic) in a grid layout. 

4)           Incubate the plate(s) at 37C 8 hours to overnight.

5)           Boil the 1.5 mL tubes for 10 minutes.

6)           Centrifuge 1 minute at 12,000 rpm.

       10 mL of the supernatant will be needed for each colony pcr rxn.

 

7)           Turn on the thermocycler, and allow it to warm up for at least 5 minutes.

 

each reaction tube needs:

1.5 mL of T7 promoter primer (in the -80oC freezer)

1.5 mL T7 terminator primer (in the -80oC freezer)

10 mL of buffer (usually A, C, F or N)

0.5 mL of Taq polymerase (in the -20oC freezer)

21.5 mL of sterile UV/UF water

10 mL of supernatant from the boiled samples

 

8)           Run “exp 001” protocol on the thermocycler

9)           When the temperature reaches 80oC, pause the program and wait 5 minutes.

10)      Add 5 mL of dNTP mix to each rxn (stir gently when adding).

11)      Resume program.

12)      When the program is complete, run 20 mL samples from each reaction on a 2% agarose gel along with a DNA ladder.

13)      Start cultures for amplifying the plasmid from those colonies showing the correct size insert.
